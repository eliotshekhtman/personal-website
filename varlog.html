OCaml Programmable Graphing Calculator

This was our final project for CS 3110: Functional Programming and Data Structures, where teams were tasked with building a system of our choosing from scratch in OCaml. My team decided to build a terminal-based programmable graphing calculator modeled off of the TI series, and to this end we developed the programming language VARLOG to allow more sophisticated scripts to be run by the calculator. As lead developer, I implemented the majority of the language functionality, including recursion, subroutines, and object-oriented functionality.
